  General Notes:

Archibald served in the Revolutionary War as a Private in the First Battalion, Somerset Co., Militia.
His gravestone, and his wife's gravestones are in the small graveyard up the hill from the Mt Horeb Church (the DAR marks them on Holidays).
His gravestone reads:
"SACRED
To the Memory of
Archibald Corring - - -
died April 19th, 1822
in the 66th year of his age
His virtue lives and ever live it must
Altho his flesh lies slumbering in the dust
Wipe off the tear, supress the swelling sigh
For he that lives aright can never die.

And on his wife Mary's grave:
MARY
Wife of
ARCHIBALD CODDINGTON
DIED 1840
Aged 84 years

This Archibald was Lora Coddington's Great Great Grandfather, and bought the farm, where she presently lives, in 1790. He built that house in about 1791.

"After the Revolutionary War Archibald must have removed from Mt Horeb to the vicinity of Black River in the northerly part of the State, for the reason that Isaiah, often told his children that his father's family removed from Black River to Mt Horeb when he was about 10 years of age, and that he helped to drive the cows when they moved, at which time he purchased the second farm east of the Mt. Horeb Church, and established the Coddington homestead. This farm is now owned by Ida M. Coddington, a great grand daughter and wife of Franklin P. Coddington." ex Coddington, Lewis M. Ancestry of a Branch of the Codington Family, p. 127
