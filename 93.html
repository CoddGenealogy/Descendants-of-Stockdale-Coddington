  General Notes:

Richard served in the New York State Militia during the Revolutionary War.
The 1790 Census of Walkill, Orange Co, New York, lists four Coddingtons heads of household in close proximity: Benjamin, Joseph, Richard, and Zachariah. They must certainly have been relatives. Two, Benjamin and Joseph, were certainly sons of Christopher 1731. Zachariah may also have been Chistopher's son, born in Pennsylvania.
Richard is unlikely to have been Christopher 1731's son because his own son was born in 1784, which implies a range for his own birth of perhaps 1750-1760. Christopher did not marry until 1753 and his first six children were born at roughly two year spacings and all baptized in Rochester.
Most of Thomas' other children did move to what is now Orange Co. New York. Richard, William, Enoch, Joseph and Benjamin, and Phoebe Jane's husband John Parshall, all fought in the Ulster Co., Militia.
The baptisms of Thomas' children have not been found. Richard is therefore plausibly a child of Thomas and brother to William, Enoch, and Joseph who went to Orange Co. in the late 1700's. However, no proof of his parentage exists.
Richard's 1790 census record lists two males over 16 (Richard and unexplained, John?), two under (Robert and Richard Jr.), and four females (Mary Osborn, Cate, Maria, unexplained). In 1800 the same family pattern repeats itself, which suggests that Richard had a son and two daughters as yet unidentified. The 1810 Wallkill census missed Richard, but he paid road taxes through at least 1812 and is absent from the 1813 roll.
