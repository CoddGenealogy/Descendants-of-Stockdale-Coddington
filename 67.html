  General Notes:

Joseph Coddington was the ancestor of many New York Coddingtons. His firstborn child Catarina (Catherine) was baptized at Rochester (Accord), Ulster Co. New York in the First Reformed Protestant Dutch Church in 1753. At New Paltz, Joseph Coddington's name first appears on the church book in 1756, when he and his wife, Catharine Vandemark, had Joseph baptized (who died as a child). At different dates up to 1774 the baptism of other children are recorded.

When the second stone church was built in 1771 Joseph Coddington performed a great amount of clerical work, every item of which is set down minutely in the church book and for which he charged £12 19s. In a document dated 1781. Jonathan LeFevre, grandfather of Hon. Jacob LeFevre, and his brother John leased for ten years to Joseph Coddington, schoolmaster, without any rent except payment of taxes, lots No. 15 and 199, being a portion of the 1,529 acres granted by letters patent to Noah Eltinge and Nathaniel LeFevre and being within the neighborhood annexed to New Paltz. Mr. Coddington was at that time becoming advanced in years and had probably concluded to give up his school, which must have been in the old stone building, now the John Drake residence, and ended his days as a farmer. We have no further information concerning Joseph Coddington, nor have we any information concerning teachers at New Paltz in the period succeeding the Revolutionary War.

The 1790 census for New Paltz lists a Joseph Coddington with one male >16, 1 under 16, and 4 females. This is more likely Joseph 1730's 5th son Joseph Jr. 1762, who did have four sons under 16. Joseph Sr. had no sons under 16 in 1791, although this implies that the enumerator missed a prominent member of the community. Although Joseph 1730 has been suggested as the parent of John Coddington (1778-), Joseph was a life-long resident of New Paltz and a member of the Dutch Reformed Church. Church records for 1778 include seven entries from Feb. 1 to Dec. 25. No John Coddington is recorded.

Served in 2nd Regiment Ulster Co. Militia, Revolutionary War. 147,148
