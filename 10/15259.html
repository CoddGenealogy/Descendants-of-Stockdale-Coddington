  General Notes:

Poplar Spring

Upon a long stretch of level land, a mile in length, upon the old Frederick Road, west of Poplar Spring, John Wayman (of Leonard and Ann Rutland), coming up from Virginia at the close of the Revolution, seated himself and built an extensive brick house in the center of his expansive survey. It became a resort of eligible young patriots many miles distant, and on Sunday afternoons the fence alon the Old Frederick Road in front had a horse standing at each corner.

John Wayman's daughters by Ann Warfield, of "Warfield's Range," were Mary, Rachel, Sarah, Amelia, and Milcah Wayman. Of the daughters only Milcah remained unmarried and stayed on the estate with her bachelor brothers Henry Wayman and Charles. Her brother John married in the West and sent one of his descndants to the United States Senate. The old estate, under Henry Wayman, was gradually reduced, by sucessive sales to Allen Dorsey and other neighbors, and after his death it was bought for a nominal price.

Source: Founders of Anne Arundel and Howard Counties. J.D. Warfield. p. 462-463. 5784
