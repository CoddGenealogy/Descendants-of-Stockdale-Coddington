  General Notes:

Attorney, House of Burgesses, Revolutionary War Soldier, William & Mary College

"...Samuel Earle, born in Westmorland county 1692, educated at William & Mary College, was a planter, attorney-at-law, member of the house of burgesses from Frederick 1746, justice, colleague of Lord Farfax 1749 to '52, collector of tobacco 1748, high sheriff, church warden 1751, and major of George William Farfax's colonial regiment. He married... Anna Sorrel, Richmond county, 1726, she being his first wife and had issue: Samuel, Baylis, John, Rachel, and Hannah... He died near the present Front Royal, Va., at a place called Earle's Ferry, on the Shenandoah River.


Samuel married Anna SORRELL, daughter of Thomas SORRELL and Elizabeth O'CANNY, on 5 May 1726 in , Richmond Co., Virginia. (Anna SORRELL was born in 1707 in , Westmoreland Co., Virginia 5039 and died on 30 Dec 1748 in , Warren Co., Virginia 5039.)
