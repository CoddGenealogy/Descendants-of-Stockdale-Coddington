  General Notes:

This family group is speculative. Edward Coddington appeared at the reunion of Sons and Daughters of Newport in 1859, listed as a printer, son of John and Hannah Wilson. Edward later shows up in the 1870 Boston census as a trade printer. Also appearing was William Coddington Davis, a clerk from Brooklyn, New York, and son of John W. and Sarah Ann Coddington.
In the 1850 Rhode Island census, Sarah Ann Coddington is a head of household with William James, 20, Charles B., and Sarah Ann, 17. A Sarah Ann also married a John W. Davis, although it seems unlikely that she would appear as a head of household under her maiden name if she had a grown son in 1859.
William J. Coddington and Eliza L. appear on the 1850 census with four children, William J., Charles B., and two daughters. It seems that William J. and Charles B. were enumerated twice, once with their parents and once in the house of their putative aunt. 5629
