  General Notes:

William was educated in the public schools of his native town, at Hasbrouck Collegiate Institute, and at the State Normal and Model School in Trenton. He read law in the offices of Suydam and Stillman and was admitted to the bar in June 1891. He served as City Judge of Plainfield, and among the many important cases with which he has been connected as counsel were those of State vs. Walsh, and Cain and Brock et al v. Borough of North Plainfield. Member of the Union Co. Tax Board. Prominent Democrat.
He was sued in 1915 by Vera Meyer DeVine for breach of promise to marry her. In response, the judge claimed that Miss Veronica Meyer had married Peter DeVine in 1902, and had never divorced him. He, on the other hand, had married his second wife before the alleged promise occurred so both the complainant and the defendant were married at the time of the alleged promise. Further, Mrs. DeVine had earlier (May 2, 1914) signed a release promising not to contact by writing, telephoning, or telegraphing Mr. Coddington, and absolving him of all actions suits, damages, etc. for the sum of $500. Mr. Coddington stated that he met Mrs. DeVine through mutual friends, never entertained her except in the company of others, and never courted her. At the time Judge Coddington was a member of the Union County Tax Board and one of the most prominent democrats in Plainfield. 855,857
