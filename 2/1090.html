  General Notes:

Died at Riveredge Farm,
William was educated in the State Normal and Model Schools in Trenton; read law with Suydam and Jackson, of Plainfield, and was admitted to the bar in February, 1883. He was active and successful in the practice of law in Plainfield, becoming one of the leaders of the Union County bar. For several years he was in partnership with John H. Jackson, the successor of the firm of Suydam and Jackson, and since Mr Jackson's death in 1894 he practiced alone. He was City Judge of Plainfield from 1889 to 1893 and then served as an attorney for Union Co.. He was counsel for several municipal and other corporations and was chairman of the Plainfield City Executive Republican Committee. In 1895 and again in 1896 he was elected to the New Jersey Assembly, where he was twice a member of the Judiciary Committee, and, during the session of 1897, Chairman of the Committee on Elections. For about ten years he was a director of the American Mutual Insurance Company of Plainfield. He was also a director of the First National Bank of that city; one of the board of managers of the Plainfield Board of Trade; a director, the counsel, and one of the organizers of the Plainfield Building and Loan Association; and a trustee of the First M. E. Church of Plainfield. (From Rev. Herbert G. Coddington, D.D.) 494,580
