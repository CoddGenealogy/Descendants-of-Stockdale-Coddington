  General Notes:

At various times during 1777, 1781, and 1782, served as a Private and Fifer and drummer under Captains Randolph and Freeman, Colonel Freeman, Colonel Ogden's New Jersey Line, Continental Army (Revolutionary War). Was both a sailor and soldier on various war vessels harassing British shipping in New York harbor and NJ coast. In 1781 he was wounded in the head in an engagement at Perth Amboy, in February, 1782 he was wounded in a skirmish at Staten Island. He was pensioned for the resulting disability from January 1, 1785.
Participated with 4-5 others in capture of the "influential" Tory, Col. Christopher Billop on 23 June, 1779. Commander of raiding party was David Coddington (?). Also with roughly same colleagues boarded and burned a British supply vessel in 1780, at Great Bend, not far from Perth Amboy. (ex. "Woodbridge and Vicinity, 1873").
Acted as a guide to American troops during "Battle of Ash Swamp."
In a letter to Freeman Worth Gardner, Marietta B. Squire wrote, "I have an interesting drawing of Robert Coddington made by his father-in-law William Inslee, from which, I have some fine Copley prints made."
Freeman Worth Gardner records that Robert and Margaret were married by the Rev. Azel Roe. 319,646,2061,2062,2063
