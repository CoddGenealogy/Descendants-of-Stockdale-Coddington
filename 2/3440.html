  General Notes:

Ronald Jay Porter relates a family tradition that his great grandfather was an Enoch G. Coddington from Mohawk Valley in New York. An "E. Gideon Coddington" appears on the 1870 census in Susquehanna Co., Pennsylvania, b. New York, with a son, Henry, aged 2. Ronald's grandfather was certainly Joseph Henry Coddington, born 1868 in Susquehanna Co. Based on this evidence, it seems likely that the "E. Gideon" of the census and his son Henry are Enoch G. Coddington and Joseph Henry of the Porter ancestors.
The link to Thomas and Naomi is not proven. In the 1860 census, Thomas' son John was enumerated in Lake Forest Twp., Susquehanna Co. on 19 Jul 1860, visit 459, and Enoch Gideon's family on visit 475 on the same day. It therefore seems likely that Enoch Gideon and John were closely related. Naomi, widow of Thomas, was enumerated with John Coddington in the 1850 and 1860 censuses. In the 1830 census, Thomas has three 20-30 year old males in his household; one could be Enoch Gideon. 2370
