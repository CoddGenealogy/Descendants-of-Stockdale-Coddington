  General Notes:

Farmer and toolmaker in his early years.

He served during the Civil War (probably never saw action). Enlisted at Flemington, NJ 3 Sep 1862 as Private, Company B, 30th NJ Infantry. Soldier's Pension No. 514,884.

Enlist Date 30 August 1862, Priv
Served New Jersey Enlisted B Co. 30th Inf Reg. NJ. Mustered Out at Flemington, NJ on 27 June 1863
Source: Register of Officers and Men of New Jersey in the Civil War 1861-65
Abbreviation: NJRoster
Published by on 1876

Following the war took his family West. They had a second son in St Louis, Missouri. Isaiah's wife (Mary) left him somewhere out in the West and brought her children back to NJ and raised them alone. Her family and that of Isaiah's supported her, and they were not pleased with Isaiah's decision to stay out West.

Died at 584 Powell St. 624,728
