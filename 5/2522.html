  General Notes:

ttingenJohn O'Rea and Mary Peck witnessed the land sale of Robert Peck and Margaret O'Rear <p35.htm> on 10 May 1708 at Richmond County, Virginia; 100 acres more or less in Stafford County to.8 John O'Rea was a planter circa 1711 at Stafford County, Virginia.9 He and Daniel Lawson O'Rear <p1.htm> received a land grant on 30 August 1711 at "Cloverfield", Prince William County, Virginia, of 400 acres on Dorrell's Run of the Occaquan River adjoining Capt. Thomas Harrison and Thomas Whitledge in what became Fauquier County, Virginia near the traditional site of Brent Town in then Stafford County from Lady Catherine Fairfax, Proprietrix of the Northern Neck of Virginia. John built the family home, "Cloverfield," there which stood until about 1940. John sold part of his land before 1744 to Col. Thomas Harrison. It was previously believed the grant was to John and his brother, but on 7 Nov 1711, John (on behalf of his infant Son, Daniel) assigned 198 acres to Thomas Botts. Thus I have changed the ownership of the grant.10 1044


John married Mary PECK, daughter of Robert PECK and Margaret, in 1700 in Dettingen Parish, Richmond Co, Virginia.1045 (Mary PECK was born in 1679 in Dettingen Parish, Richmond Co, Virginia 1044,1045.)
